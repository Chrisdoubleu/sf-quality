## 1. Executive Summary

**Bottom line: Can a quality engineer trace a customer complaint back through this system?**

**No. Not reliably.**

You can sometimes reconstruct a story inside the paint department (carrier/rack on some inspection sheets, some process parameters on trackers), but the trace chain breaks hard at two points:

**Shipping → Production:** Pack slips and shipping trackers do not carry any consistent run identifier (no rack/carrier ID, no paint date/shift, no lot number). Example: `8.2.2.4.2 P2 General Pack Slip Template.xls` and customer pack slips like `8.2.2.4.12 Rollstamp BMW Packslip Template.xlsx` show part + quantity + date, but nothing that ties to a paint run.
- *Evidence:* `8.2.2.4 Shipping Forms/8.2.2.4.2 P2 General Pack Slip Template.xls` (Sheet1); `8.2.2.4.12 Rollstamp BMW Packslip Template.xlsx` (Sheet1).
- *Confidence:* High.

**Post-paint rework loop:** Sanding/buffing/deburring is where defects go to die. The tally sheets capture time and counts, not defect cause, not source run, not final disposition/outcome.
- *Evidence:* `8.2.2.7 Daily Sanding Tally Sheet.xlsx` (1st); `8.2.2.8 Daily Buffing Tally Sheet.xlsx` (Days); `8.2.2.9 Daily Deburring Tally Sheet.xlsx` (1st).
- *Confidence:* High.

**Why Plant 2 is slightly worse overall:**

**Better than Plant 1 in a few pockets:** Some forms actually capture spray parameters and viscosity/color verification (liquid paint reality).
- *Evidence:* `8.2.2.3.5.5 Metelix Hummer Spoiler Application Tracker.xlsx` (Sheet1); `8.2.2.2.15 102 Line Paint Production Tracking.xlsx` (Sheet1); `8.2.2.26 New Paint Batch Verification.xlsx` (Sheet1).

**But Plant 2 is drowning in uncontrolled documents:** 205 obsolete vs 84 current (2.4:1 churn), and 32/84 current forms stale (>2 years old). Most current forms have no visible revision/effective date control (only 11/84 had any revision evidence detectable).
- *Evidence basis:* `ai_indexes/forms_manifest.csv` + in-file header checks where present.
- *Confidence:* High.

**And Plant 2 has a new failure mode Plant 1 didn't:** Post-paint rework black hole (sanding/buffing/deburring) creates "rework without closure". This is uniquely severe for liquid paint.

### Key Findings (Blunt)

**No formal NCR system in the forms.** Hold tags exist, but there's no numbered NCR record tying containment → investigation → disposition.
- *Evidence:* `8.2.2.12 Hold Tag.xlsx` and `8.2.2.13 Hold For Review.xlsx` are labels, not NCRs.
- *Confidence:* High.

**Defects are captured, but only as "embedded columns"** in customer inspection sheets and a couple of application trackers. There are zero dedicated defect tracking forms in the current set.
- *Evidence:* Current inventory categories + inspection forms.
- *Confidence:* High.

**Defect naming is still inconsistent.** 78 unique raw defect strings across current forms normalize down to ~47 candidate defects before mapping to the platform's seeded taxonomy. Plant 1 had 97 raw strings → 38 mapped types; Plant 2 is smaller, but still messy.
- *Evidence:* Inspection/application trackers.
- *Confidence:* High.

**Disposition is implied, not controlled.** "Good / Buff / Sand & repaint / Scrap" exists on several inspection sheets, but there's no consistent mapping to the platform's 11 DispositionCodes and no linkage to NCR.
- *Evidence:* Multiple customer inspection forms.
- *Confidence:* High.

**Shipping is noisy but not traceable.** 16 shipping forms/templates/trackers create admin activity, not traceable product genealogy.
- *Confidence:* High.

### A. Quality Inspection Forms

**1) What checks/tests are performed?**

Almost all "inspection forms" are visual defect tallies + rework routing. They capture: Good quantity, Buff quantity + buff defect types (dirt/fiber/etc), Repaint/rework quantity + defect types (thin clear, thin paint, sag/run, etc). Some include molding/substrate defects (flash, splay, dents, cracks, sink marks).

*Evidence:* See Embedded Defect Capture Matrix rows above. *Confidence:* High.

**2) ASTM / test method references?**

None found in the current set (no ASTM D3359, D523, etc in inspected spreadsheets). This plant is not documenting adhesion/gloss/film build test methods in these forms.

*Evidence:* Inspection workbooks + keyword scan across current forms; no ASTM strings found. *Confidence:* High.

**3) Accept/reject decision field?**

Decision is implicit via buckets: "Good / Buff / Sand & repaint / Scrap" and "Return to buff / Send to rework". Not a single consistent "Accept/Reject" decision field.

*Evidence:* Mytox Inspection Form.xls, Laval Tool Inspection.xlsx, Metelix Buff Inspection.xlsx. *Confidence:* High.

**4) Measurements captured as readings or pass/fail?**

Inspection sheets are mostly counts, not measurements. The only real numeric metrology found in current files is in paint batch verification (ΔE, Lab\*). That's lab-side, not product inspection.

*Evidence:* `8.2.2.26 New Paint Batch Verification.xlsx`. *Confidence:* High.

**5) Batch/lot/rack concept tying inspection to run?**

Inconsistent:
- Rollstamp BMW: Rack # field exists.
- Mytox: Rack # field exists.
- Metelix application tracker: Carrier # exists.
- Most other inspection sheets: no carrier/rack ID.

*Evidence:* 8.2.2.3.1.1 (BMW), 8.2.2.3.3.1 (Mytox), 8.2.2.3.5.5 (Metelix tracker) vs others. *Confidence:* High.

**6) How different are customer-specific forms?**

They're mostly the same skeleton with customer branding and a few defect columns swapped:
- Laval / KB / Misc share a common template family.
- Tesla forms are unique due to molding defect taxonomy and revision log tab.
- Metelix has the most complex stack (paint + buff + sanding/molding inspections).

*Evidence:* Side-by-side structure of those forms. *Confidence:* High.

**7) Buff inspection forms — structurally different?**

For Laval and KB: not really; it's the same table repeated as a "second-stage" sheet. For Metelix and Tesla: yes, buff inspection introduces unique defect modes (haze, snowballs, burn-through, foam marks).

*Evidence:* 8.2.2.3.4.2, 8.2.2.3.6.2 vs 8.2.2.3.5.2, 8.2.2.3.7.2. *Confidence:* High.

**8) Cross-plant comparison (Plant 1 had 31 inspection forms vs Plant 2 ~14 current):**

Plant 2 is not necessarily "inspecting less" — it's consolidating multiple programs into fewer templates and pushing some inspection into application trackers. But the tradeoff is worse standardization and linkage.

*Evidence:* Multi-program sheets like Laval "KANBAN SHEET", Tesla multiple tabs, Metelix tracker. *Confidence:* Medium-High.

**9) InspectionRecord entity validation (proposed from Plant 1):**

Confirmed, but must support "count-based defect tallies" and "multi-stage inspection". Plant 2 inspection data is not test-result style; it's defect-count routing data.

### B. Production Tracking Forms

**1) Can you trace raw receipt → load → paint → unload → post-paint → packaging?**

No. You can trace pieces of it, but the chain is broken:
- Receiving log tracks PO receipt and accept/reject (8.2.2.4.1).
- Load sheet tracks daily loads (no rack IDs) (8.2.2.1.17).
- Some lines track carrier/rack IDs (102 loaded parts + Metelix tracker).
- Post-paint tally sheets don't link to runs.
- Pack slips don't link to runs.

*Confidence:* High.

**2) Consistent identifier tying load → tracking → inspection?**

No consistent enterprise identifier. You have: Carrier # (some forms), Rack # (some forms), Paint date/shift (many forms). But these are not consistently carried across forms.

*Evidence:* 8.2.2.2.12 (carrier), 8.2.2.3.1.1 (rack), 8.2.2.4.\* (shipping lacks both). *Confidence:* High.

**3) Are quantities tracked at load AND unload? Yield possible?**
